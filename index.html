Online Shockwave Detection in Traffic Video

M.Sc. thesis research at Maastricht University. Designed and trained CNN and LSTM deep learning architectures to detect and track shockwaves (stop-and-go waves) in real-time vehicular traffic video streams. Traffic Flow Heterogeneity from Video Data

M.Sc. internship project at Maastricht University. Built a complete computer vision pipeline to extract traffic flow heterogeneity metrics from surveillance footage. The system detects and classifies individual vehicles, tracks their trajectories across frames, and computes flow-level statistics such as headway distributions, speed variance, and lane-changing rates. These metrics provide richer input for traffic models than traditional aggregated sensor data.

Multi-Scale Traffic Flow Modelling Framework

Ph.D. research at TU Delft (2011–2016). Developed a unified framework that seamlessly couples microscopic (individual vehicle), mesoscopic (platoon-level), and macroscopic (flow-level) traffic models. The framework automatically selects the appropriate resolution for each section of a network, enabling city-scale simulations with corridor-level detail where needed. Published 7 peer-reviewed papers; nominated for Best Paper at IEEE ITSC 2013. Includes an emergency evacuation simulation of Tehran City and bicycle modelling in SUMO.

Material Property Identification via Kalman Filter

M.Sc. thesis at Ruhr University Bochum. Developed a method for identifying material properties of structures using an Extended Kalman Filter coupled with Finite Element Method simulations. The approach estimates unknown material parameters (stiffness, damping) from noisy sensor measurements by iteratively updating a state estimate. Applied to structural health monitoring scenarios where direct material testing is impractical.
